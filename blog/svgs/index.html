The object element is currently the best way to add an svg. This article by Josh Sortino has the best explaination of creation.

The important things to remember are to have a fall back image for shitty browsers and to remove any width and height attributes, bc then why make an svg?

The biggest pain in the ass, and the reason i am writing this, is making your svg clickable. Example, you want you logo to be scalable and your home button. You will have a headache trying to do this. Thanks to Alan Johnson from Treehouse for relieving my headache! I tried:

  1. wrapping the object in my a tag
  2. wrapping the whole div in my a tag
  3. z-indexing
  4. absolute positioning
  5. adding the link in the xml (this is was the common solution i found, however click the lonely cockroach and see)

The answer! = wrap the object in the a tag, give the a tag a position: relative; display: inline-block; and use the after pseudo element on a with bottom: 0; content: ""; left: 0; position: absolute; right: 0; top: 0;
